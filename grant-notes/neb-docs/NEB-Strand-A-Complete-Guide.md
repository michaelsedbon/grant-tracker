# NEB Prizes 2026 — Complete Strand A (Champions) Guide

**Downloaded:** 2026-03-17

## Overview

The New European Bauhaus (NEB) Prizes recognise inspiring projects and ideas that improve people's quality of life by combining sustainability, inclusion and beauty.

- **Strand A** rewards Champions: mature, implemented projects that are already delivering concrete results on the ground.
- **Strand B** supports Rising Stars: promising ideas or early-stage initiatives, often led by young talents, with strong potential for impact.

In 2026, the Prizes will feature:
- One special thematic prize on **Water Resilience** (€20,000)
- **International dimension**: applications from Moldova, Brazil, and Japan
- **13 prizes** of up to €20,000 to winners
- **14 runners-up** each receive €5,000

**Deadline: 22 March 2026 at 19:00 CET** (extended from 17 March)

---

## What Strand A (Champions) is for

Strand A (Champions) awards **existing, completed projects** that are already implemented and can show positive results.

## Who can apply?

Applications are open to **individuals and organisations** (public, private, or non-profit), provided the project is implemented in the EU, the Western Balkans, Moldova, or Ukraine.

## What the prizes aim to recognise

Visibility to existing projects integrating the three NEB core values (sustainability, aesthetics and inclusiveness) and working principles (participatory process, multi-level engagement, transdisciplinary approach).

---

## Prize Amounts (Strand A – Champions)

- **5 winners** in Strand A each receive **€20,000** (1 per category + 1 public vote winner)
- **Runners-up** receive **€5,000** each
- **1 Water Resilience** special prize: **€20,000**

Winners also receive an **Accelerator Programme**: communication package, tailored coaching, strategic guidance, and an assigned official Coach.

## The Four Categories

### Category 1 — Enhancing Circularity, Sustainability, and Innovation

Rewards projects combining beauty, sustainability, and social inclusion through circular and resource-efficient approaches. Eligible projects apply circular principles (reuse, repair, modularity, bio-based materials). May include the built environment, energy/water/material-efficient strategies, nature-based solutions, digital/AI tools.

### Category 2 — Strengthening Local Democracy and Inclusion

Rewards projects that strengthen local democracy, social cohesion, and community trust by placing citizens at the centre. Must demonstrate genuine participatory and co-design approaches. May use digital platforms and immersive environments. Addresses vulnerable groups.

### Category 3 — Arts, Culture, and Heritage as Drivers of Change

Rewards projects using arts, culture, and cultural heritage as drivers of creativity, innovation, and social transformation.

**Welcomes:**
- Transdisciplinary initiatives connecting **art, science, and industry**
- Embedding artists/cultural professionals in **technological and scientific environments**
- Collaboration with academic, construction, and manufacturing sectors
- Combining cultural knowledge with **digital and emerging technologies**
- Physical transformation of places connecting local heritage with contemporary lifestyles
- Development of **products, services, or business models** rooted in local culture (fashion, furniture, interior design, food, everyday living)
- Culture-led initiatives creating meaningful, inclusive, enriching experiences

### Category 4 — Enablers for New European Bauhaus Transformation

Recognises projects creating conditions to develop, scale up, and mainstream NEB solutions. Includes governance models, funding schemes, education programmes, experimental environments, peer-learning platforms, professional training.

## How to Apply

- Submit via the [NEB Prizes platform](https://prizes.new-european-bauhaus.europa.eu/)
- Application period: 20 Feb 2026 – **22 March 2026 at 19:00 CET**
- Each application: **one category, one strand only**
- Applicants may submit multiple applications for **different projects**
- Applications **cannot be modified after submission**
- Requires an **EU Login** account
- Must include at least **6 photographs** with copyright info

See **Annex 1** for guiding questions.

## Eligibility Criteria

### Table 1: Who you are
1. Individuals, public authorities, non-profits, or private entities
2. EU or non-EU residents (project must be in EU/WB/Moldova/Ukraine)
3. Not subject to EU restricted measures
4. Must be entitled to represent the project
5. Not in exclusion situations (Article 136)
6. Assumes sole liability

### Table 2: Nature of your project
1. Implemented in EU, Western Balkans, Moldova, or Ukraine
2. Cross-country cooperation encouraged (lead partner identified)
3. **No double EU monetary prizes** (grants are OK)
4. May resubmit if didn't win previously
5. Must explain NEB values and working principles integration
6. Related to a **specific location** with clear target group and objectives
7. **Must be replicable** in other contexts

### Table 3: How you apply
1. Legal representative name indicated
2. Category and strand clearly indicated
3. Submitted by deadline via online platform
4. **In English**
5. Same project cannot be in multiple categories/strands
6. Multiple submissions allowed if different projects
7. Fully completed form with all mandatory fields + signed Privacy Statement
8. At least **6 photographs** with copyright info
9. Must include documentation of results (evaluation report, project report, certifications)

### Maturity requirement
Projects in Strand A must be **fully completed** at time of application. Processes, tools, business models, or methods fully developed. Must have been implemented in at least one specific case.

## Award Criteria (100 points total)

### a) Results & Impact (10 points)
- Showing results, outputs, or impact
- Benefits to direct and indirect beneficiaries
- Transformative potential from global perspective

### b) NEB Core Values — Exemplary Character (40 points)
1. **Sustainability** (environmental): reducing environmental impact, advancing circularity, nature-based solutions, biodiversity, lifecycle thinking
2. **Inclusion**: accessibility, affordability, celebrating diversity, intergenerational interaction, supporting vulnerable communities
3. **Aesthetics & Quality of Experience**: design quality, cultural benefits, sense of belonging, meaningful experiences, connection to place

### c) Working Principles — Exemplary Character (35 points)
1. **Participatory process**: community involvement in co-design, decision-making, implementation
2. **Multi-level engagement**: horizontal (citizens, stakeholders, municipalities) and vertical (regional, national)
3. **Transdisciplinary approach**: engagement across fields + non-academic knowledge + public

### d) Innovation (5 points)
Novel approaches vs. mainstream/business-as-usual practices

### e) EU Competitiveness (5 points)
Soundness of the financial approach

### f) Replicability (5 points)
Potential for transferring methodology, solutions, experiences to various places

### Additional jury criteria (for final ranking):
- Geographical diversity
- Balance between "hard" (built environment) and "soft" investments
- Diversity of contexts (urban, rural, urban-rural)

## Selection Timeline

1. **March 2026** — Eligibility check
2. **April 2026** — Quality assessment by external experts (2 experts per application)
3. **June 2026** — Online public voting (list of finalists published)
4. **June 2026** — Final jury assessment
5. **July-September 2026** — Winners selected and notified

**Finalists:** 8 in Strand A (at least 2 per category) + 8 in Strand B + 5 for water resilience + 6 international

Minimum quality threshold: **50% of maximum aggregate score**
